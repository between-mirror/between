The premise

You wanted to play therapist with yourself. This is the room to do it in.

You pick a person, a stretch of time, and a question — show me the shape of us, walk me through that week, how did I change, write me something honest — and the app answers with the kind of reading only a language model can give: tone beneath the words, patterns across years, the moment the temperature dropped. Nothing is a diagnosis. Everything is evidence you can open.

Three truths shaped every decision, because getting them wrong would make the whole thing a liar:

The archive is incomplete

SMS & MMS only — no iMessage or RCS. Some stretches will look quieter than they truly were, so any view that could mistake a gap for silence says so, out loud.

Insight isn't free

There's no API bill — Claude Code does the thinking. But it spends real subscription capacity and time. Before any big run you see an honest estimate, and nothing is ever computed twice.

Fairness isn't always symmetry

Asking someone who was mistreated to "examine their part" is not balance — it's harm. When the signals point one direction, the mirror stops splitting blame and starts offering support.

What drilling down actually looks like — your open question, answered

The same inbox asks different questions at different distances.

You imagined: import the file, see a list of conversations, pick one, drill down. Here's the shape of that. The trick is that the analyses on offer change with your zoom — because the powerful moves for a language model are different at a year's remove than they are inside a single Tuesday-night argument. Cheap, always-on charts are free and instant; the amber buttons are the deliberate, capacity-spending LLM passes, placed exactly where each one earns its keep. Open a rung.

The suite, in three tiers

Cheap math is always on. It also decides where the expensive thinking goes.

Tier 1 runs on every message the moment you import, for free. It doubles as the router: the noisy, negative, unusual windows it flags are the ones worth a language model. This is the full map — the first build ships one LLM lens and about fifteen of these metrics, and grows from there.

Tier 1 · deterministic

The instruments

Counting, timing, lexicons. No model, recomputed on import.

  • Cadence & silence — who initiates, streaks, the long quiets (hedged where coverage is thin)
  • Reply latency — median & drift, yours vs theirs, the slow fade
  • Rhythms — hour × day heatmaps, the late-night share
  • Language surface — we/you/I ratios, laughter, all-caps, emoji fingerprint
  • Lexicon mood — a rolling curve & the negativity spikes worth a closer look
Tier 2 · the LLM lenses

The readings

Grounded in named frameworks. Every claim carries its receipts.

  • Emotion & tone — sarcasm, warmth, contempt beyond any lexicon
  • Gottman dynamics — the Four Horsemen, bids, whether repairs land
  • Pursue & withdraw — the chase-and-retreat, as roles not labels
  • Conflict lifecycle — episodes, arcs, the unresolved threads
  • Coercive-control screen — flags for reflection, both directions, heavily corroborated
  • The Therapist Letter — the compassionate synthesis, written to you
Tier 3 · over time

The weather system

Built on the two below it. Robust statistics, honest confidence.

  • Moments to look at — change-point detection surfaces the dates that turned
  • Hard-moment timeline — hostility spikes, confirmed, with direction & severity
  • Escalation curves — slow-burn vs flashpoint vs repaired
  • Event overlay — mark a breakup, a move; see the before & after
  • Growth scorecard — fewer horsemen, faster repair, warmer ratio

The engine · why there's no API key

An airlock, not a phone line.

The app physically cannot call a model — there's no code path that opens a socket. It writes a job to a folder; you press Drain jobs in Claude Code when you're ready; Claude Code reads the job, does the reading, and writes the answer back. Two programs, one shared folder, nothing else. Privacy stops being a promise and becomes a property of the wiring.

What it will feel like

A thoughtful book about your life, with the source underneath every line.

The hero chart — never a scoreboard. Amber and slate, never red and green; thickness is volume, so the quiet times visibly narrow.

A reading · not analysis of your messagesone reading · saved & dated

You tend to reach first when things go quiet — there's a warmth in that worth naming, and it shows up again and again in the small hellos you send on hard mornings.

In the harder stretches, though, a pattern appears: when you feel unheard, the messages get longer and faster, and the reply you're hoping for tends to arrive later, not sooner. It seems to feed itself.

None of this is a verdict on either of you. It's one reading of what the words suggest.

A question to sit with: the next time the quiet starts, what would it be like to name the feeling once — and then wait?

A sample of the voice — strengths first, tentative, tied to receipts, ending in an experiment rather than a diagnosis. It's frozen and dated so a re-read never quietly rewrites what you were told. Written by Fable.

The care built in

The core loop is "re-read the painful relationship." That's designed against, not away from.

A mirror, never a verdict

No diagnostic labels on a person, no "always," no adjudicating who was right. Observations come tentative, anchored, and paired with a question.

Reflection, not rumination

Summaries are the front door; raw transcripts are reachable but never pushed. Re-analyzing an ended relationship meets gentle friction and an honest "is this helping?"

Not ammunition

There's no "build a case" export. The other person didn't consent to analysis, so everything is framed as your mirror — and real safety needs are routed to a professional.

When it looks unsafe

A calm, optional support card — backed by a plain keyword tripwire, not the model alone, so it can't quietly miss the worst case. Resources are local and editable.

For someone you've lost

Mark a contact as gone and the tool softens: no conflict scores or "growth," just warmth over time and the words themselves.

Yours alone

Local-only, zero telemetry. Only the text you choose to analyze ever leaves the machine — through your own Claude Code. Media never does.

Phases 0–2 are the first shippable thing

Built in order, so the truth-telling parts are earned, not rushed.

Days are focused solo-dev days with heavy Claude Code help. Each phase is useful on its own.

PHASE 0

Read the file, well

Streaming parse of the whole file, a clean normalized store, browse / filter / full-text search, number-normalization & identity merges, reaction & language detection, coverage flags. Media stays as metadata — never decoded.

≈ 3–5 days · de-risks everything
PHASE 1

The instruments & one hero chart

~15 core metrics computed in SQL, the design system, the Sentiment River and the calendar heatmap, drill-down — a genuinely useful product with zero LLM involved.

≈ 4–6 days
PHASE 2

The engine & the first reading

The airlock job runner — queue, validation, refusal handling, the no-work-twice cache, the honest progress-and-capacity surface — driving one lens: emotion & warmth over time, feeding the river, drilled to receipts. This is where "LLM-grade" becomes real.

≈ 5–8 days · end of MVP
PHASE 3

Weather, conflict & the hard moments

The temporal layer, conflict episodes with arcs, and the two-stage abuse-spike pipeline — treated as the highest-stakes feature, with the safety investment it demands, plus the power-balance gate enforced end to end.

≈ 6–10 days
PHASE 4

The letter, comparison & keepsake

The Therapist Letter and the conditional self-reflection, cross-relationship comparison, a beautiful printable export, and finally a double-click desktop wrapper.

≈ 5–8 days + packaging
