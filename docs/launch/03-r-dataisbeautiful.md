## Read this before posting

- **Check the current rules.** They change. As of writing: `[OC]` in the title, and a **first comment
  from the poster** giving the data source and the tools used. Missing that comment gets the post

**Title:**

> [OC] Two years of a relationship's emotional temperature, from a synthetic SMS archive

---

**First comment (post immediately, by the author):**

> **Data:** entirely synthetic. This is a fictional couple ("Alex & Jordan") generated by a seeded
> script — 787 invented messages over 26 months, written to be an ordinary hard-but-loving
> relationship: a warm start, friction over chores and money and time, and repair after most fights.
> No real person's messages are involved, and none ever will be; the tool that produced this is
> local-first and its author's own archive is deliberately never the demo.
>
> **What you are looking at:** each day is a column. Amber above the line is warmth, slate below is
> tension, and thickness is how much was said that day. The scores come from a language model reading
> each message once; the chart itself is hand-drawn to canvas, no charting library.
>
> **Tools:** TypeScript, SQLite, a hand-written canvas renderer. Colour is deliberately amber/slate
> rather than red/green — this is emotional data, not pass/fail, and green-for-good would be a
> judgement the chart has no business making.
>
> **Caveat worth stating:** texts carry less than half of any relationship. Everything said in a room,
> on a phone, or in silence is missing, so a chart like this systematically under-represents whoever
> did their repairing in person.
>
> Source, if useful: <https://github.com/between-mirror/between> (AGPL).
