# Launch drafts — not posted, not scheduled, not approved

**Nothing in this folder has been posted anywhere, and nothing here should be posted by anyone but
Michael.** These are drafts written ahead of time so that the writing is not being done under the
pressure of a launch day. Posting is an owner act (task bus #187), and it is gated on the Era-2 kit
being genuinely live: the landing page, the interactive demo a stranger can click, and the video.

**Where that gate stands.** The landing page is live, and as of v0.4.0 so is the interactive demo —
[between-mirror.github.io/between/demo](https://between-mirror.github.io/between/demo/), the real
application on a fictional archive, no install, no account, no request to any other origin. Every
draft below may now link to it directly rather than asking a reader to clone and run a command, which
is the single biggest thing standing between a curious stranger and actually seeing the tool. **The
video is the one remaining piece of the kit**, and it is Michael's to record or approve.

## Rules that apply to every one of them

These come from [SHIP.md](../SHIP.md) §3 and [VOICE.md](../VOICE.md), and they are not stylistic
preferences — each of them is a way this project could do real harm if it got the tone wrong.

1. **Never**: AI therapist · relationship judge · abuse detector · evidence generator · couples app.
   Not as a claim, not as a joke, not in a headline.
2. **Never market on anyone's crisis.** The 988 surface is a floor, not a feature, and it is never a
   selling point.
3. **Say the scope precisely**: "for people with an Android SMS/MMS archive". Not "your messages",
   not "your chats" — until more importers exist, the wider phrasing is a lie of omission to every
   iPhone user who reads it.
4. **Feedback, not sales.** The soft round exists to find out what strangers think and what breaks.
   There is nothing to buy, and the posts should not read as though there is.
5. **Read the venue's live rules before posting.** They change. r/selfhosted requires the megathread
   for new projects; r/dataisbeautiful requires `[OC]` plus a first comment disclosing data source and
   tools. Being the person who ignored the sidebar is a bad first impression that cannot be undone.
6. **Only Alex & Jordan appear.** Every screenshot, every chart, every number. The author's own thread
   is user zero and is never the demo, the screenshot, or the case study.
7. **Disclose the synthetic data every time**, unprompted, in the post itself — not only when asked.
8. **Lead with the limitation** wherever the venue is technical. The audiences worth having will find
   the gap anyway, and finding it themselves after being told it wasn't there is how a project loses
   them permanently.
