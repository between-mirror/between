# Storyboard — the 60–90 second demonstration

**Status: NOT RECORDED.** For Michael to record or approve. Everything on screen is **Alex & Jordan**,
the fictional couple — never a real archive, in this or any other recording, permanently.

**Length:** 75 seconds is the target; 90 is the ceiling.
**Voice:** none. Text on screen and the interface's own motion. A voiceover makes it an advert; silence
makes it a demonstration, and this tool's whole argument is *look at the thing itself*.
**Music:** none, or something almost inaudible. Do not score someone's relationship.
**Capture:** 1440×900, light theme, the same window the docs visuals use (`npm run demo:serve`).
**Cursor:** visible and slow. Every click should be legible as a decision.

---

### Beat 1 · 0:00–0:06 — the archive, unglamorous

A terminal. One command: `npm run demo:serve`. Cut to the browser opening on the conversation list —
one name, *Jordan*, 787 messages, 2022–2024.

> **Caption:** This is a fictional couple. Your own archive never leaves your computer.

*Why first: the trust claim arrives before the pretty part, not after it.*

---

### Beat 2 · 0:06–0:20 — the years, at once

Click *Jordan*. Home opens; the river draws across 26 months — amber above, slate below. Let it sit.
No cuts for four full seconds. This is the emotional beat of the whole video and rushing it wastes it.

> **Caption:** Two years, as weather. Warmth above the line, tension below.

---

### Beat 3 · 0:20–0:32 — it is made of real counting

Scroll gently to the report card: who reaches first, reply time, conversations, longest quiet.

> **Caption:** All of it counted, not guessed. Free, offline, forever.

---

### Beat 4 · 0:32–0:44 — the seasons

Explore → Eras. The named stretches scroll past: *New love · Building a life · The friction of close
quarters · A quieter, harder stretch.*

> **Caption:** The arc, in its seasons.

*Do not linger on the hostility percentages. The names are the story here; the numbers are the
evidence, and they get their moment in the next beat.*

---

### Beat 5 · 0:44–0:58 — **the beat that matters**

Readings → a first reading. Scroll to an observation. Click the claim. The receipt opens and the
actual message appears underneath it, with *"That's not right"* beside it. Hold for three seconds.

> **Caption:** Every observation opens to the messages it came from.
> **Second caption, after a beat:** If it's wrong, you say so.

*If only one beat survives an edit, it is this one. It is the entire difference between this and a
horoscope.*

---

### Beat 6 · 0:58–1:06 — it refuses

Ask → type *did we fight about money?* → the honest decline: *The archive doesn't hold enough to
answer that honestly.*

> **Caption:** And when the words don't support an answer, it says so.

*Showing the tool failing to answer is the most persuasive thing in the video. Do not cut this because
it looks like a bug — caption it so it clearly is not.*

---

### Beat 7 · 1:06–1:14 — your choice, your machine

Settings → the engine chooser. Cursor rests on **Local-only**, then the *Your data* tab: the real
database path, its size, and *Back up now* / *Delete all Between data*.

> **Caption:** Local by default. You can back it up, or delete all of it, from inside the app.

---

### Beat 8 · 1:14–1:20 — the close

Cut to a still card on the paper background:

> **Between Mirror**
> Read your own archive — with the words underneath.
> Free and open source · No accounts · No telemetry
> github.com/between-mirror/between

Hold four seconds. No call to action beyond the address; nothing to buy.

---

## Rules for the recording

1. **Alex & Jordan only.** If a real name, number, or message appears in a single frame, the take is
   destroyed rather than trimmed.
2. **No crisis content.** The demo contains none by construction; do not go looking for the heaviest
   thing on screen.
3. **No claim the tool does not make.** No "understand your relationship", no "find out what went
   wrong", no "AI-powered". The captions above are the script.
4. **Real time.** Do not speed up a loading state to look faster than it is. If something is slow,
   either cut around it honestly or leave it.
5. **No red or green** anywhere in the titling. Amber, slate, clay — the same palette as the
   application.
