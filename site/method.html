Method

What is counted, what is inferred, and where the line between them is drawn — because the second kind of statement is only worth reading if you can tell it from the first.

Two layers, deliberately separate

The deterministic layer is arithmetic over your messages: volume, timing, who opened a conversation, reply latency, word and emoji frequencies, day and hour rhythms, bounded episodes of tension, era segmentation. No model is involved. It is free, instant, offline, and it is the same numbers every time.

The interpretive layer is a language model reading the actual text: emotional texture per message, and prose readings composed from that. It costs time or money, it is opt-in, and it is never the only thing on screen.

Receipts, mechanically

The model does not write prose. It returns typed blocks, and each block that makes a claim must carry the ids of the messages it rests on. At render time every id is resolved against the real database; a block whose evidence does not resolve is dropped rather than shown. The application composes the final text from the survivors.

The sentences that connect paragraphs, and the question that closes a reading, assert nothing — so they cannot carry receipts, and the model is not allowed to write them either. They come from a small set of fixed, authored lines chosen by a hash of the reading's own content. The result is that nothing in a reading is both unreceipted and meaningful.

What a receipt is, and what it is not. A receipt shows where an observation came from. It does not prove that the observation is the only reasonable interpretation.

This is the limit of the whole method, and it is worth being plain about. The evidence contract stops the model quoting messages you never sent and asserting things it read nowhere. It cannot stop a true message being cherry-picked, a sarcastic line being read flat, a private joke being taken at face value, or three examples standing in for six years. It cannot see the calls, the RCS and iMessage threads, or the conversations you had in the kitchen — and what is missing shapes a reading more than what is present. The application's Archive health report quantifies that gap before it shows you any pattern.

So the receipts are not there to prove the reading right. They are there so you can find the place where it is wrong — which is the only thing a machine can honestly offer about a relationship it was not in.

Coverage is shown, and it gates the chart

An unscored message reads as neutral. So a thread the model has only partly read does not look partly read — it looks calm, which is the most misleading thing this software could do. Below 95% coverage the chart draws the deterministic layer instead, and says so under the chart. Above it, it says that too. Stretches the model declined are named as declined, not left as gaps.

Calibration, and why it asks you to be harsh

Before a directional reading means much, the tool is tuned to your judgement: you label a blind sample of your own thread, both sides, without seeing what the model thought. It then measures whether you were gentler on your own hard messages than on the other person's — the common human tilt — and holds the frame more neutral when you were.

Until that is done, directional readings say plainly that they are provisional.

Where it refuses

  • Below the evidence floor, it declines rather than stretching thin evidence into a full reading.
  • Asked something the archive cannot support, it says so instead of answering.
  • No diagnosis, ever. No clinical nouns applied to a person. Behaviour is described in the words that were used.
  • No prediction, no prescription, no verdict. Interpretations are offered with an alternative reading beside them, because two things are usually true at once.
  • The abuse-pattern layer is a research preview, disabled in ordinary builds, and not externally validated. There is no setting for it; the running program cannot switch it on. It will not be marketed as detecting abuse before independent clinical review, or after.

The limit worth stating twice

Texts carry less than half of any conversation. What happened in the room, on the phone, and in the silence is not in the archive — and a tool that reads only the text will systematically miss whoever did their repairing in person. Every reading says so on its face.
