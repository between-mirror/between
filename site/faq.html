Questions

Including the ones with uncomfortable answers.

Do my messages get uploaded?

The counting never leaves your machine. The written readings need a language model, and you choose whether that is a local one (nothing leaves), your Claude subscription, or your own API key — with the cost shown before anything runs. The default sends nothing. Details.

Can I use it on my iPhone messages?

Not yet, and there is no way to fake it: the parser has no path for an iPhone backup, and iMessage traffic is not in an Android SMS archive at all. If both of you used iPhones, the archive does not exist. iMessage and WhatsApp are the next major version.

Will it tell me who was right?

No, and it is built specifically not to. It will show you what was said, when, how often, and by whom — and where it offers an interpretation it offers an alternative one beside it. If you want a verdict, this is the wrong tool, and that is a design decision rather than a missing feature.

Can I use it to prove something in court, or in an argument?

Please do not. Exports are deliberately neutral — the verbatim messages and a hash, with none of the tool's readings attached. There is no "build a case" mode and there will not be one. A tool that reads two people's private messages and then hands one of them ammunition is a different and much worse product.

Does it detect abuse?

No. There is an experimental layer that describes coercive patterns in behavioural language, and it is off by default, text-only, dependent on your own calibration, and not externally validated. It will not be marketed as abuse detection before independent clinical review — or after. If you are trying to work out whether you are safe, please talk to a person; there is a list of confidential lines in the project's support document.

Who should not use this?

Anyone looking for evidence against a partner. Anyone who wants to be told they were right. Anyone currently in crisis — this is a slow, quiet tool for reflection, and it is not a substitute for a person. And anyone reading someone else's archive: it is built for the owner of the messages, and nothing about it is designed for surveillance.

What happens to my data if the project stops?

Nothing. Your database is a plain SQLite file on your own disk, and the source is AGPL — it keeps working with no server to shut down, no licence to expire, and no account to lose. That is what local-first actually buys you.

Is it finished?

No. The deterministic instrument works end to end and is well tested. The interpretive layer is experimental and gated. The installer does not exist yet. STATUS.md is the authoritative list, including the things that are currently broken or slow.

Why is it free if it took this long to build?

Because charging for the analysis would mean putting a price on someone's own history, and because the cost to serve you is zero — there is no server. Money is only ever for convenience: an installer for people who should not have to use a terminal. Pricing.

Can I fork it and make my own version?

Yes. That is what the licence is for. Please just do not call it Between Mirror or imply it is the official build — when software reads someone's private messages, being able to tell whose build you are running is the one thing that matters. The terms.
