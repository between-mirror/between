Pricing

The software is free and always will be. What is planned for sale is a trusted distribution of it — not features, and never the analysis.

Nothing is for sale yet. There is no checkout on this site and no way to pay. These are the planned prices, published early so nobody is surprised later.

Community — free, forever

The full source under the AGPL. Every current analysis feature, with no product-imposed usage limits. Manual install: Node, a terminal, five minutes.

Official Desktop — $29 founding

The first hundred buyers. A signed one-click installer with guided import, automatic migrations, signed updates, safe data management, and 30 days of setup support.

Official Desktop — $49

After the first hundred. One payment, not a subscription. All version-1 updates and security fixes for as long as it is supported.

What you would be paying for

Exactly one thing: not having to be a developer. The installer, the guided first run, the migrations, the updates that do not break your database, and someone answering when it goes wrong. The core features and analysis remain the same open-source code. The paid distribution adds signed packaging, guided setup, updates and support — not exclusive insights or stronger privacy.

Between Mirror has no archive-hosting or per-analysis infrastructure cost. The installer price funds packaging, signing, compatibility testing, updates and human support — not access to the user's own data or analysis.

What you would not be paying for

  • Not the analysis. The deterministic instrument is free in both editions, forever. There is no reading a paying user gets that a source user does not.
  • Not per message, per reading, or per month. One payment. No meter.
  • Not a licence check. No activation, no accounts, no hardware fingerprint, no entitlement phone-home. The installer can be shared; the AGPL allows it and so do we. What you get is a build whose origin you can verify. The only request that build will make on its own is the identifier-free update check — switchable off, and knowing nothing about who is asking.
  • Not privacy. Privacy is not a tier: nothing on this site is withheld from the free version. One wrinkle worth stating rather than glossing — OS-level containment of the model drain becomes real only in the packaged app, and today both paths have the weaker tool-level containment. That is a property of being packaged, not of being paid: the packaging source is AGPL like everything else, so anyone who builds it gets the same boundary. What the money buys is not having to build it.

Why publish a price for something not on sale

Because the alternative is a waitlist that hides the number until you are invested. If $49 is too much, that is worth knowing now, and the free version is a real answer rather than a consolation prize.

The model costs are yours either way and are not ours to charge for: a full emotion pass over a large archive is roughly $30 on your own API key, or free and slower on a local model. The written readings after that are pennies. Our cost to serve you is zero, because we serve you nothing — which is the entire point.

Sponsoring maintenance

Separate from the waitlist, and separate from ever buying anything: some people would rather fund the upkeep of a tool they use than wait for a product to exist. Maintenance is the unglamorous half — security patches, a Node version moving under us, an Android export format changing shape — and it is the half that decides whether this still works in three years.

There is nowhere to send money yet. GitHub Sponsors is not enabled on the organisation, so any "sponsor" button here today would be decoration. When it is open, this section becomes the link and this paragraph goes away. Until then the genuinely useful contributions are a bug report with a synthetic repro, a fresh-install attempt on hardware we do not have, or telling us where the documentation lied to you.

Sponsorship will never buy influence over what the tool says about anyone's messages, a vote on what gets built, access to anyone's data, or a feature withheld from the source. Small acknowledgements that cost nobody anything — a development note, an early look at a build whose code is already public — are the most it will ever come with. It funds upkeep of software everyone already has.
